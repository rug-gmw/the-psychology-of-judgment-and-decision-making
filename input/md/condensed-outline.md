# Foundations

## Reason and Intuition
Two systems underlie judgment and decision-making: **System 1** (fast, automatic, associative) and **System 2** (slow, deliberate, rule-based). **Bounded rationality** explains why System 1 dominates — not laziness, but cognitive necessity. Key concepts: **dual-process theory**, **heuristics**, **ecological rationality**, **categorization**, **Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT)**.

## Bayesian Reasoning
Rational belief updating combines **prior beliefs** with new **evidence** to yield a **posterior**, formalized in **Bayes' theorem**. Most biases in the book are specific departures from this ideal. Key concepts: **base rates**, **likelihoods**, **natural frequencies**, **calibration**, **signal detection theory**, **ideal observer models**.

## Heuristics and Biases
**Heuristics** are mental shortcuts that substitute a hard question for an easier one, often producing systematic **cognitive biases** — analogous to perceptual illusions. Key concepts: **affect heuristic**, **bias blind spot**, **debiasing**, **bounded rationality**.

## How It Is vs. How It Should Be
Three model types frame the field: **descriptive** (how people actually decide), **normative** (how they ideally should), and **prescriptive** (practical recommendations bridging the two). Bayesian updating serves as the normative standard throughout the book.

## Mental Models
People reason using layered background knowledge: **framework theories**, **stances** (mechanical, design, intentional), **schemas**, and **scripts**. The adopted stance activates different priors and shapes causal reasoning, memory, and moral judgment. Key concepts: **artifacts vs. natural kinds**, **prediction error**, **schema-driven inference**.

# Intelligence

## What Is Intelligence?
Intelligence is best understood statistically rather than verbally. The **g-factor** (general intelligence) predicts performance across diverse cognitive tasks. Key concepts: **fluid intelligence** (novel problem-solving, declines with age), **crystallized intelligence** (accumulated knowledge, increases with age), **IQ**, **s-factors**.

## Intelligence vs. Rationality
High intelligence does not guarantee good reasoning. **Dysrationalia** describes adequate intelligence paired with poor thinking dispositions. Key concepts: **Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT)**, **Actively Open-Minded Thinking (AOT)**, the distinction between computational *capacity* and the *disposition* to use it.

## Intelligence in the Real World
**General Mental Ability (GMA)** is among the strongest single predictors of academic and occupational success, especially in complex jobs. Key concepts: **twin studies**, **Flynn effect** (20th-century IQ rise), **reversal of the Flynn effect**, **predictive validity**.

# Uncertainty

## Availability
People estimate frequency or probability by how easily instances come to mind, producing systematic distortions. Key concepts: **availability heuristic**, **accessibility**, **negativity bias**, **media influence**, **biased evidence sampling**.

## Representativeness
Probability judgments are driven by resemblance to a prototype rather than statistical information, causing **base-rate neglect**. Key concepts: **conjunction fallacy** (Linda problem), **stereotyping**, **gambler's fallacy**, **hot-hand belief**, **prior vs. likelihood**.

## Anchoring and Primacy
Estimates and impressions are anchored on an initial value or first piece of information, with insufficient adjustment. Key concepts: **anchoring heuristic**, **primacy effect**, **cross-modal anchoring**, **framing**, **real estate and sentencing applications**.

## Overconfidence
Overconfidence manifests as a **self-favorable bias** (shifted posterior: overestimation, better-than-average effect, unrealistic optimism) and **overprecision** (narrowed posterior: underestimating uncertainty). Key concepts: **planning fallacy**, **Dunning-Kruger effect**, **depressive realism**, **calibration**, **feedback and expertise**.

# Causality

## Cues to Causality
People infer causation from situational features rather than rigorous analysis. Key concepts: **causal field**, **temporal order**, **contiguity**, **covariation**, **causal mechanism**, **similarity of cause and effect**, **counterfactual reasoning**, **simulation heuristic**.

## Blame, Praise, and Responsibility
When the potential cause is a person, **intentional stance** reasoning triggers attributions of blame or praise. Key concepts: **personal vs. impersonal causality**, **covariation principle** (consensus, distinctiveness, consistency), **correspondence bias / fundamental attribution error**.

## Learning Causality
Causal relationships are learned through association and **prediction error**. Key concepts: **classical and operant conditioning**, **blocking**, **illusory correlation**, **illusory causation**, **regression toward the mean**, **cell A bias**, **plausibility as prior**, **experimental vs. correlational designs**.

# Past and Future

## Hindsight
After learning an outcome, people believe they "knew it all along" — **creeping determinism**. Key concepts: **hindsight bias**, **memory and hypothetical paradigms**, **curse of knowledge**, **outcome bias**, **causal narrative construction**, **reverse hindsight bias**.

## Statistical vs. Intuitive Prediction
Across domains, **statistical prediction** consistently outperforms **clinical intuition**, yet professionals resist statistical methods. Key concepts: **actuarial vs. clinical judgment**, **feedback loops**, **structured assessment**, illustrated with judicial selection and data-driven football recruitment.

## The Future Self
People mispredict how future events will feel (**affective forecasting**) and undervalue outcomes to their future self (**temporal discounting**). Key concepts: **impact bias**, **focalism**, **projection bias**, **hot-cold empathy gap**, **peak-end rule**, **disability paradox**, **future self as a stranger**.

## Risk Perception
Risk judgments are driven by affect and heuristics, not probabilities. Key concepts: **voluntary vs. involuntary risk**, **dread risk**, **scope insensitivity**, **identifiable victim effect**, **relative vs. absolute risk framing**, **natural frequencies in risk communication**.

# Choice

## Gains and Losses
People evaluate outcomes relative to a **reference point**, not in absolute terms. Key concepts: **prospect theory**, **loss aversion**, **diminishing sensitivity**, **probability weighting function**, **certainty effect**, **endowment effect**, **framing effects**, **ambiguity aversion**, **omission bias**.

## Satisficing vs. Maximizing
People differ in whether they seek the best option (**maximizing**) or a good-enough one (**satisficing**). Maximizers achieve better outcomes but feel worse. Key concepts: **bounded rationality**, **choice overload**, **Maximization Scale**, **regret**, **exploration-exploitation tradeoff**.

## Steering Other People's Choices
Every choice environment is designed and inevitably influences behavior. Key concepts: **libertarian paternalism**, **nudges**, **defaults**, **status quo bias**, **mental accounting**, **fungibility**, **commitment devices**, **framing**, **social norms**.

## Group Decisions
Groups can outperform or dramatically underperform individuals depending on process. Key concepts: **wisdom of crowds** (independence, aggregation), **group polarization**, **groupthink**, **shared information bias**, **altruistic punishment**.

# Belief

## Confirmation
People preferentially seek, interpret, and remember information that confirms existing beliefs. Key concepts: **confirmation bias / myside bias**, **positive test strategy**, **falsifiability**, **2-4-6 task**, **asymmetric updating**, **exploration-exploitation tradeoff**.

## Belief Formation and Perseverance
Beliefs form automatically (**Spinozan acceptance**) and resist correction. Key concepts: **belief perseverance**, **continued influence effect**, **myth-versus-fact effect**, **reactance**, **cognitive dissonance**, **sunk cost**, **rebound effect**.

## Conspiracy Theories
Conspiracy theories are self-sealing belief systems that absorb contradictory evidence as confirmation. Key concepts: **apophenia**, **agency detection**, **echo chambers**, **group polarization**, **rejection of science**, **unfalsifiability**, **social and existential functions**.

---

# Morality and Cooperation

## Morality
Moral judgments arise primarily from fast **intuitions** (System 1), with reasoning serving as post-hoc justification. Key concepts: **social intuitionist model**, **moral dumbfounding**, **Moral Foundations Theory**, **somatic markers**, **affect-as-information**, **trolley vs. footbridge dilemma**, **omission bias**, **Kohlberg's stages**.

## Cooperation
Social dilemmas pit individual rationality against collective welfare. Key concepts: **tragedy of the commons**, **prisoner's dilemma**, **ultimatum game**, **public goods game**, **altruistic punishment**, **intuitive cooperation** (System 1 default), **framing effects on cooperation**.

# Real Life

## Medical Decision-Making
Clinicians are subject to the same heuristics and biases as anyone, with life-or-death consequences. Key concepts: **availability in diagnosis**, **anchoring**, **confirmation bias**, **decision support systems**, **natural frequencies**, **causal models of disease**.

## Legal Decision-Making
Police, forensic experts, and judges make consequential decisions under uncertainty and cognitive bias. Key concepts: **forensic confirmation bias**, **anchoring in sentencing**, **representativeness and racial bias**, **story model**, **temporal order of evidence**, **information integration theory**.

## Populism and Social Media
Social media amplifies populist messages and misinformation through feedback loops with algorithms and human psychology. Key concepts: **loss framing**, **negativity bias**, **moral outrage**, **echo chambers**, **Spinozan acceptance**, **availability manipulation**, **algorithmic amplification**.

## Artificial Intelligence
LLMs mirror many features of human cognition, including its biases. Key concepts: **Turing test**, **System 1/2 analogy** (chain-of-thought as System 2), **crystallized vs. fluid intelligence**, **hallucination vs. confabulation**, **inherited human biases**, **calibration**, **sycophancy**.
